01 — About

I work at the messy, interesting edge of robotics — where a box of humanoid parts meets a developer who needs a demo running in an hour.

My day job is hardware support engineering: diagnosing, repairing, and shipping humanoid robots. The undocumented half of the role is software triage, logistics, and flying to conferences to run the booth as a technical salesperson.

Before robotics I ran organoid cultures at UCSC's Genomics Institute, wrote JEDEC manuals and a thousand-line VB forecasting tool at Adesto, and spent most of my weekends winning hackathons with friends.

Selected hardware projects

Liquid Hacks · 1st Place Overall

R.A.N.G.E / Valorant Reloaded

A modded Nerf blaster stuffed with an Arduino Pro Micro and two MPU‑6050 accelerometers, paired with a Dance‑Dance‑Revolution‑style wooden foot pad. Together they replace the mouse and keyboard — the blaster is the cursor, the pad is your legs, and an Oculus Quest running Virtual Desktop puts you inside the game.

My part: designed, built, and integrated the circuitry inside the blaster and the foot pad. On‑the‑fly hardware debugging.
Pinnacle 2021 · Invited Hackathon

NotWheels

A custom toy‑car pipeline — upload any STL into our three.js web app, align it to a purpose‑built 1:64‑scale base, and print yourself a Hot Wheels with literally anything riding on top. The core trick was designing a base geometry that handled wheel axles cleanly across arbitrary user models.

My part: 3D modeling for the base and printed parts, OctoPrint setup, final assembly across the whole batch.
